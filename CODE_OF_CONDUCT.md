# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders of **Mi9chada CTF Team** pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community — both online and at competitions.

---

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

- **Respect competition integrity**: Never share flags/solutions during active CTFs
- **Practice responsible disclosure**: Report vulnerabilities in our tools/repos privately via email.
- **Credit original authors**: When reusing writeups, tools, or challenges from others
- **Be respectful**: Use welcoming language, value differing viewpoints, and gracefully accept constructive criticism
- **Focus on learning**: Help junior members grow; avoid gatekeeping or elitism
- **Respect privacy**: Never dox players, share personal information, or violate competition NDAs

### Unacceptable Behavior

- **Competition sabotage**: Flag hoarding, false flag submissions, or attacking other teams' infrastructure without explicit permission
- **Live flag sharing**: Publishing solutions/flags during ongoing competitions
- **Harassment**: Offensive comments, intimidation, stalking, or unwelcome attention
- **Discrimination**: Exclusionary behavior based on identity or background
- **Security violations**: 
  - Testing exploits against live CTF platforms without permission
  - Sharing credentials, API keys, or sensitive infrastructure details in public repos
  - Submitting malicious code (backdoors, cryptominers) in PRs
- **Plagiarism**: Presenting others' writeups/tools as your own without attribution
- **Disrespect toward organizers**: Ignoring competition rules or platform terms of service

---

## Competition-Specific Guidelines

| Scenario | Expected Action |
|----------|-----------------|
| **During active CTF** | Keep all solutions/flags private until competition ends |
| **Found vulnerability in CTF platform** | Report privately to organizers — never exploit for advantage |
| **Teammate shares live flag publicly** | Immediately DM them to delete + report to team captain |
| **Opposing team asks for hint** | Decline politely: *"Good luck — see you on the scoreboard!"* |
| **Competition has NDA** | Never publish writeups without explicit organizer permission |

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders (team captains and faculty advisors) are responsible for:
- Clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior
- Taking appropriate and fair corrective action in response to violations
- Moderating GitHub issues/PRs, Discord channels, and competition conduct
- Protecting privacy of reporters and those accused until investigations conclude

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:
- All repositories under `Mi9chada-CTF-Team` organization
- GitHub issues, PRs, comments, and discussions
- Team Discord/communication channels
- In-person events (UM6P competitions, cybersecurity weeks)
- Interactions with other teams during competitions
- Social media representation of Mi9chada

---

## Reporting Violations

###  For Security Issues (vulnerabilities in tools/repos)
→ Report in an email, either the team mail or contact directly one of the core members.

### For Code of Conduct Violations

Contact one of the core members


All reports will be handled with:
-  **Confidentiality**: Your identity will not be shared without consent
-  **Timeliness**: Acknowledgment within 24 hours, resolution within 7 days
-  **Fairness**: Both reporter and reported party will be heard

---

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these steps when addressing violations:

### 1. Correction
**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or unprofessional behavior  
**Consequence**: Private warning + clarification of expectations

### 2. Warning
**Community Impact**: Single or repeated unacceptable behavior  
**Consequence**: 
- Formal written warning
- Temporary restriction from GitHub org (1–7 days)
- Required apology before reinstatement

### 3. Temporary Ban
**Community Impact**: Serious violation or sustained inappropriate behavior  
**Consequence**:
- Ban from org/repos for 30–90 days
- Removal from competition lineups
- Required meeting with faculty advisor before return

### 4. Permanent Ban
**Community Impact**: 
- Pattern of violations
- Harassment, doxxing, or threats
- Competition sabotage (e.g., attacking other teams)
- Malicious code submission  
**Consequence**: Permanent removal from organization and UM6P ACM CTF activities
